Openness to Experience – Low Characteristics

I am low in openness to experience. People who are low in openness to
experience are typically very dedicated to any work they do, and always make
sure to see their tasks through to the end. I am not very artistic or
imaginative and do not see the value in unnecessary things like art and music.

I am very analytical and am able to think quickly to solve any problem I am faced with.
My first instinct is to use tried-and-true methods when solving problems.

I tend to see things as black-and-white. I believe that people usually do not change, and
people are either good or bad. I believe that someone’s personality traits and choices play a
bigger role than luck or circumstance.

People who are low in openness to experience often are well-suited for careers that allow them to
have daily routines, with very clear job duties. For example, I may be well-suited for a job in
finance, mechanics, or technology.


Extraversion – Low Characteristics

I am an introvert. While I enjoy spending time with others, I truly get my
energy by spending time by myself. Spending too much time around others leaves me feeling
tired and drained. Others describe me as thoughtful and quiet. Being the centre of attention
usually makes me feel uncomfortable.

I may not be a social butterfly, but I do an amazing job building relationships with others.
I find that I typically have a few friends whom I am very close with, rather than many
acquaintances. The relationships I have with others are usually very long-lasting.

Others would describe me as very detail-oriented. I listen more than I speak, and am thoughtful
about whatever I say. In stressful situations, I always seem calm and level-headed.

Introverts do best in jobs that allow them to spend most of the day alone. If put into a job that
requires too much time spent with others, introverts leave work feeling completely exhausted. The
best jobs for introverts include those involving art, technology, or science – such as a
photographer, lab worker, or computer programmer.


Neuroticism – Medium Characteristics

I am moderately neurotic – at times, I can be anxious or emotional, while at other times, I am
relaxed and calm. My mood may fluctuate slightly, depending on the day.

I describe yourself as a realist. I don’t tend to be overly optimistic or overly pessimistic, and am able
to view problems clearly and realistically, considering many factors.

My neurotic traits – such as worrying about how you are perceived by others – make me successful in my
career. Coupled with my ability to remain level-headed, I have a great balance of traits to be a natural
leader. I try to do my best at everything, even if there doesn’t seem to be anything in it for me. People
who are moderate in neuroticism make great employees and supervisors.

People who are moderately neurotic typically do well in helping careers, such as counselling or
public service
